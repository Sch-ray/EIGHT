Welcome, and thank you for your support!

If you have a 3D printer, you can now print a more beautiful ⭐ and personalized 😎 enclosure for your device! If you have enough time, you can also choose finer print settings for even better quality.

I printed the enclosure using **PETG**. With a properly calibrated printer, the print quality is quite good.

> [!WARNING]
> **Warning:** Disassembling the enclosure may damage your device. Any risks or damage resulting from the disassembly are entirely your own responsibility.

### Before You Print Your Own Enclosure

1. Disassembly and reassembly involve certain risks. Please read this guide carefully and make sure you have a soldering iron and steady hands.
2. Replacing the enclosure requires some hands-on experience. Before you begin, make sure you are confident you can complete the process without damaging your device. Any damage caused during disassembly is your own responsibility.
3. Make sure your 3D printer's build plate is clean and has good adhesion. Printing the front shell with embossed text requires excellent first-layer adhesion. (When I printed the first prototypes, a dirty build plate caused many failed prints.)
4. Be prepared for some handmade imperfections inside the device. Since each unit is assembled and soldered by hand, you may notice some signs of manual workmanship. 😄

### 3D Printing

You can print the enclosure you need from the [Shell_Model](././Shell_Model) directory. The folder with the highest version number contains the latest design.

### Disassembly and Reassembly

> [!TIP]
> Before disassembling the device, touch a grounded metal object nearby or wear an anti-static wrist strap to prevent electrostatic discharge (ESD) from damaging the PCB.

**1. Remove the Top Cover**

On the back of the device, you will see four obvious screws. Remove them using a screwdriver.

![Step_1](./Step/IMG_1076.jpg)

You will then have access to the internal PCB.

![Step_2](./Step/IMG_1077.jpg)

**2. Disconnect the Cables**

Disconnect the battery connector.

Also, **don't forget to remove the microSD (TF) card** before continuing.

**3. Remove the PCB**

Lift the PCB upward from the antenna connector side first.

Once lifted, the PCB can be removed from the enclosure.

![Step_3](./Step/IMG_1078.jpg)

![Step_4](./Step/IMG_1079.jpg)

**4. Remove the Remaining Components**

The enclosure also contains four metal heat-set inserts.

If you want to reuse them in your new enclosure, heat them with a soldering iron (or another suitable tool) and carefully pry them out.

![Step_5](./Step/IMG_0726.jpg)

One final detail:

On the side of the enclosure are three small openings for the status LEDs. I originally used tiny glass beads as transparent "windows."

You may choose to reuse them by gently heating them during removal and installation.

However, because the glass beads are extremely small and somewhat difficult to handle, I recommend simply filling the openings with a small amount of white or transparent glue instead. Melted plastic can also be used if preferred.

![Step_6](./Step/IMG_1080.jpg)

**5、Last Step**
Now simply perform the above steps in reverse order to install everything into your new enclosure.
